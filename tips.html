Brown Butter M&M Cookies

Tips

To guarentee successful browned butter:

  • Use a Light-Colored Pan: A light-colored skillet allows you to see the butter’s color change more clearly, helping you catch the perfect browning point without burning it.
  • Watch for the Nutty Aroma: As the butter browns, it will develop a rich, nutty aroma. This scent is a key indicator that it’s nearing the desired color. Pay close attention, as it can go from perfectly browned to burnt quickly.
  • Remove from Heat Promptly: Once the butter reaches a golden brown color, remove it from the heat immediately. Residual heat will continue to cook it, so transferring it to a cool bowl can help stop the cooking process.
  • Strain if Desired: If you want to remove the browned milk solids for a smoother finish, you can strain the butter through a fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth. This will leave you with clarified brown butter, which can be used in various recipes.

Brown Butter Phases

The sticks of butter will begin to melt.

The butter will start to produce white bubbles.

The butter will start to form larger bubbles and start to boil.

The white bubbles will start to go away as the butter continues to boil. You should start to see brown bits around the side of your pan. Turn the heat down.

After the heat is turned down, the butter will produce frothy, white bubbles. Make sure to stir and try to see if the butter has fully browned yet.

Once the butter has browned, transfer to a glass bowl and place in the fridge/freezer to cool.
